\section{Exact Runtime Formulae}
\label{app:sec:exact-runtime}

For mutation probability $p$, the exact expected optimization time on
\textsc{LeadingOnes} is~\cite{bottcher2010optimal}
\[
  \mathbb{E}[T_p] = \frac{1}{2p^2}\bigl((1-p)^{-n+1} - (1-p)\bigr),
\]
minimized at $p \approx 1.59/n$, giving $\approx 0.77n^2$. For
\textsc{OneMax}, the
sharp expansion at $p = 1/n$ is~\cite{hwang2018probabilistic}
\[
  \mathbb{E}[T] = e\,n\ln n - 1.8925\ldots\, n + \tfrac{e}{2}\ln n + O(1).
\]
More generally, for $p = c/n$ the expected time on any linear function is
$(1\pm o(1))\frac{e^c}{c}\,n\ln n$, minimized at $c = 1$~\cite{witt2013tight}.

\section{Implementation-Aware Variants}
\label{app:sec:impl-aware}

A practical observation of \citet{pinto2018practice}
is that roughly a
$1/e$ fraction of all $(1+1)$-\acrshort{ea} iterations flip \emph{no}
bit (the offspring equals
the parent), yet standard analyses charge a full function evaluation
for each such
iteration. Real implementations avoid these wasted evaluations by one
of two strategies.

\paragraph{Resampling variant -- $(1+1)$-\texorpdfstring{EA$_{>0}$}{EA>0}.}
\label{app:par:resampea}
The mutation strength $\ell$ is drawn from the \emph{conditional} binomial
distribution $\mathrm{Bin}_{>0}(n,p)$, i.e., $\mathrm{Bin}(n,p)$ conditioned on
$\ell > 0$. This effectively skips any iteration that would flip nothing.

\paragraph{Shift variant -- $(1+1)$-\texorpdfstring{EA$_{0\to1}$}{EA0to1}.}
\label{app:par:shiftea}
If $\ell = 0$ is sampled, it is replaced by $\ell = 1$, shifting all
probability mass
from zero flips to exactly one flip.

Both variants are analyzed in full
in~\cite{pinto2018practice,jansen2011analysis}. The key runtime
consequences with $p = 1/n$ are summarized in
Table~\ref{app:tab:variant-runtimes}.

\begin{table}[h]
  \centering
  \begin{tabular}{lccc}
    \hline
    \textbf{Algorithm} & \textbf{OneMax} & \textbf{LeadingOnes} \\
    \hline
    $(1+1)$-EA         & $e\,n\ln n + O(n) \approx 2.718\,n\ln n$ &
    $\approx 0.859\,n^2$ \\
    $(1+1)$-EA$_{>0}$  & $\approx 1.718\,n\ln n + O(n)$           &
    $\approx 0.543\,n^2$ \\
    $(1+1)$-EA$_{0\to1}$ & $\approx 1.359\,n\ln n + O(n)$         &
    $\approx 0.517\,n^2$ \\
    RLS                & $n H_n \approx n\ln n$                    &
    $\approx 0.5\,n^2$   \\
    \hline
  \end{tabular}
  \caption{Expected optimization times at $p = 1/n$ for the $(1+1)$-EA and its
    implementation-aware variants. RLS is included for reference.
    Bounds for the variants follow
  from~\cite{pinto2018practice,bottcher2010optimal,witt2013tight}.}
  \label{app:tab:variant-runtimes}
\end{table}

\noindent Resampling or shifting yields a 35--47\% reduction in
function evaluations on
\textsc{OneMax} and roughly 37--40\% on \textsc{LeadingOnes} at $n = 600$, as
confirmed empirically in~\cite{pinto2018practice}. For the (1+1)~EA$_{>0}$, the
expected runtime on any linear function with $p = c/n$ becomes
$(1\pm o(1))\frac{e^{c-1}}{c}\,n\ln n$~\cite{pinto2018practice}, a
multiplicative
$(1-e^{-c})$ improvement over the standard algorithm.
